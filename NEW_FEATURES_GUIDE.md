## üß™ SOIL ANALYSIS

### Quick Start (4 Steps)

1. **Go to Farm** ‚Üí Click farm name
2. **Click "Soil Analysis"** (yellow button)
3. **Enter Measurements:**
   - pH Level (use test kit)
   - Nitrogen (N)
   - Phosphorus (P)
   - Potassium (K)
4. **View Results** ‚Üí Automatic analysis with recommendations

### What You Need

**Required:**
- pH meter or test kit (KES 500-2000)
- NPK test kit OR lab results

**Where to Get Testing:**
- Agricultural stores (test kits)
- County agricultural office (FREE)
- Private labs (KES 2000-5000)

### What You'll See

‚úÖ **Overall Health**: Excellent, Good, Fair, or Poor  
‚úÖ **pH Status**: Acidic, Neutral, or Alkaline  
‚úÖ **NPK Levels**: Low, Medium, or High for each  
‚úÖ **Recommendations**: What to add to your soil  
‚úÖ **History**: Track changes over time  

### Example Results

**Good Soil**
- pH: 6.5 (Slightly Acidic) ‚úì
- Nitrogen: 55 ppm (Medium) ‚úì
- Phosphorus: 45 ppm (Medium) ‚úì
- Potassium: 60 ppm (High) ‚úì
- Health: EXCELLENT
- Action: Maintain current levels

**Poor Soil**
- pH: 4.8 (Too Acidic) ‚úó
- Nitrogen: 20 ppm (Low) ‚úó
- Phosphorus: 15 ppm (Low) ‚úó
- Potassium: 25 ppm (Low) ‚úó

## üí° TIPS FOR BEST RESULTS

### Climate Classification
1. Allow location access when browser asks
2. Try search if GPS doesn't work
3. Use specific place names (e.g., "Kisii Town" not just "Kisii")
4. Check multiple locations to compare

### Soil Analysis
1. Take samples from 5-10 spots in your farm
2. Mix samples together for average
3. Test before planting season
4. Retest every 6-12 months
5. Keep records to track improvements
